About Me

I'm a PhD candidate in a joint program between the University of Adelaide and the University of Copenhagen, working on understanding how species respond to global change. My research combines my passion for ecology, birds, and computational methods to tackle some of the most pressing questions in conservation biology.

Research Focus

My doctoral research uses process-based modelling to identify species traits that confer resilience in the face of climate and land-use change. Specifically, I investigate how these large-scale environmental changes have shaped European bird populations over recent decades and explore the mechanisms by which species characteristics mediate their distributional responses. By integrating ecological theory with computational approaches, I aim to predict the future persistence of bird species under scenarios of continued global change.

Research Interests

Beyond my core PhD research, I'm interested in macroecology, biogeography, species distribution modelling, and the broader implications of trait-based approaches in understanding ecological resilience.
