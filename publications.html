Publications

My research publications and contributions to the scientific literature, primarily focused on ecology, biogeography, and conservation.

Research Focus Areas

My publications span several interconnected research areas, including:

  • Macroecology and Biogeography: Understanding large-scale patterns of species distribution and biodiversity
  • Species Distribution Modelling: Predictive approaches to understand where species can persist
  • Climate and Land-Use Change: Impacts on wildlife populations and ecosystem dynamics
  • Avian Ecology: Ecological responses of bird populations to environmental change
  • Trait-Based Approaches: Using species characteristics to predict responses to global change
  • Machine Learning in Conservation: Deep learning applications for species identification and biodiversity monitoring

Current Research Projects

PhD Research: Process-based modelling of European bird responses to climate and land-use change, with emphasis on understanding how species traits mediate distributional shifts and promote resilience under global environmental change.

Collaborations: Development of deep learning applications for real-time classification of medically relevant freshwater snail species, supporting research on schistosomiasis and other waterborne diseases.
